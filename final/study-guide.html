3 Regression

You should be able to:

  • Interpret the slope (change in Y for 1-unit change in X)
  • Interpret the intercept (predicted Y when X = 0)
  • Make predictions using a regression equation
  • Interpret R¬≤ (percentage of variation explained)
  • Calculate a residual (Actual - Predicted)
  • Explain what Lasso regression does (shrinks coefficients, helps feature selection)

Key formulas:

Predicted Y = Intercept + Slope √ó X
Residual = Actual Y - Predicted Y
üí°

R¬≤ = 0.72 means 72% of the variation in Y is explained by the model. The other 28% is unexplained (residual variation).

Review: Regression notebooks, scikit-learn examples

4 Decision Trees & Random Forest

You should be able to:

  • Follow a decision tree to make a prediction
  • Understand that the first split uses the most important feature
  • Explain what overfitting means (high training accuracy, low test accuracy)
  • Explain why limiting tree depth improves generalization
  • Explain what a Random Forest is (ensemble of many trees)
  • Understand why ensembles perform better (reduce variance, more robust)
üí°

A single deep tree memorizes training data (overfits). Random Forest builds many trees on random subsets and averages ‚Äî this reduces overfitting.

Review: r2d3.us visual intro, ensemble methods notebook

5 Decision Analysis

You should be able to:

  • Read a decision tree with probabilities and payoffs
  • Calculate expected value at chance nodes
  • Apply Bayes' theorem to update probabilities
  • Determine the optimal decision strategy
  • Understand the value of information

Key concepts (from Global Petroleum):

Prior: P(Oil) = 0.30
Conditional: P(Positive | Oil) = 0.80
Joint: P(Positive AND Oil) = P(Positive | Oil) √ó P(Oil)
Posterior: P(Oil | Positive) = P(Oil AND Positive) / P(Positive)
üí°

Bayes' theorem lets you update your beliefs with new evidence. The seismic test changes P(Oil) from 30% to 77% if positive, or down to 8% if negative.

Review: Global Petroleum Corporation exercise ‚Äî understand each step

6 Data Handling & Visualization

You should be able to:

  • Understand basic Pandas operations (loading, selecting, filtering)
  • Interpret a scatter plot (direction, strength, outliers)
  • Interpret a correlation heatmap (which variables relate?)
  • Know when to use Seaborn for visualization
üí°

In a heatmap: dark colors = strong correlation, light = weak. Red/blue often indicate positive/negative.

Review: Pandas notebooks, visualization examples

7 Train/Test Split & Overfitting

You should be able to:

  • Explain why we split data into training and test sets
  • Recognize overfitting (great on training, poor on test)
  • Recognize underfitting (poor on both)
  • Explain how Lasso or limiting tree depth prevents overfitting
üí°

A model that memorizes training data won't work on new data. Test data simulates "the future" ‚Äî it tells you if your model generalizes.

Review: Train/test split in scikit-learn notebooks
